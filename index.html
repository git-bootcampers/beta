Group 4: Kawal Ng Lupa

Pepito was walking somewhere around the barren dessert of Calachuchi.

Seamlessly lost, he looked at his enchanted kettle and felt shocked.

He walked over to the window and reflected on his deserted surroundings.

He had always loved dirty Cape Town with its expensive, eager estuaries.

It was a place that encouraged his tendency to feel shocked.

Then he saw something in the distance, or rather someone.

It was the figure of Digong. Digong was a noble giant with ugly eyes and feathery hands.

Pepito gulped, he glanced at his own reflection. He was a afraid and shocked.

His friends saw him as a blushing, breezy bear.

Once, he had even jumped into a river and saved an amused owl.

But not even a hopeful person, he was prepared for what digong had in store today.

The moon shone like sleeping pigeons, making pepito anxious.

As pepito stepped outside and digong came closer, he could see the husky glint in her eye.

"I am here because I want justice", digong bellowed, in a smart tone.

She slammed her fist against pepito's chest, with the force of 356 koalas. "I frigging hate you PEPITO!!"

pepito looked back, even more anxious and still fingering the enchanted kettle.

"DIGONG!!, exterminate", he replied.

They looked at each other with ambivalent feelings, like two filthy, flabby foxes sitting at a very violent funeral, which had flute music playing in the background and two intelligent uncles gyrating to the beat.

pepito regarded digong's ugly eyes and feathery hands. He held out his hand. "Let's not fight", he whispered, gently.

"Hmph", pondered digong.

"Please?", begged pepito with puppy dog eyes.

digong looked surprised, her body blushing like a high-pitched, hilarious hawk.

Then digong came inside for a nice drink of beer.


THE END
